Abstract

Industrial fires involve flammable materials, high-pressure equipment, and complex layouts, making efficient and safe suppression essential. Firefighting robots have great potential, but their effectiveness depends on precise visual perception. However, existing methods treat fire detection, flame segmentation, and water-jet landingpoint localization as separate tasks and lack a unified, collaborative perception framework. In addition, the accuracy of current techniques for each task is still insufficient for practical deployment. To address this problem, we propose FireJet, a video-stream-based coupled visual firefighting system that integrates all three key tasks within a single framework. FireJet adopts an edge-cloud collaborative architecture: the robot uses an improved YOLO-Fire model for lightweight fire detection, while the cloud performs highprecision flame segmentation and water-jet landing-point localization based on SAM2 with automatic prompt generation. These outputs provide detailed visual information for downstream decision-making and control. Experiments in industrial-like scenarios show that FireJet achieves stable and accurate multi-task visual perception, offering a unified and reliable visual foundation for closed-loop firefighting control.
